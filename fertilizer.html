Understanding NPK

N - Nitrogen

Role: Responsible for the growth of leaves on the plant.

Deficiency Symptoms: Yellowing of older leaves, stunted plant growth.

Common Source: Urea (46% N)

P - Phosphorus

Role: Crucial for root development and flower/fruit production.

Deficiency Symptoms: Dark green or purplish leaves, poor root system.

Common Source: DAP (18% N, 46% P)

K - Potassium

Role: Helps with the overall functions of the plant to perform correctly (disease resistance).

Deficiency Symptoms: Brown scorching and curling of leaf tips.

Common Source: MOP (60% K)

Safety Guidelines for Application

  • Timing: Apply fertilizers during early morning or late evening. Avoid application during heavy rains to prevent runoff.
  • Placement: For basal doses (DAP/MOP), place the fertilizer 2-3 inches below the seed. Do not let seeds touch chemical fertilizers directly.
  • Moisture: Ensure adequate soil moisture before applying Urea to prevent volatilization (nitrogen escaping as ammonia gas).
  • Protective Gear: Always wear gloves and a mask when handling chemical fertilizers to prevent skin irritation and inhalation of dust.
